AI Dictionary Paperlight Design System
00 · Creative north star

Paperlight

A calm sheet of paper-light in the margin of whatever you're reading. AI Dictionary explains a word in this sentence, in your language, then gets out of the way. The system is tuned for tired eyes on long reads: gentle, warm-shifted surfaces, type that's clear without being harsh, and a single quiet accent that signals rather than shouts.

01 · Themes

Two reading climates, one set of tokens

Themes are token sets, not redesigns. Components read only semantic --ad-* names; a theme re-binds those names. Switching is a single attribute — data-ad-theme — driven by the picker in Settings and persisted. Use the toggle up top to flip this entire page.

◐  Sepia — default, reading-warm

Aged-paper surface, warm brown-grey ink. The everyday climate: bright enough to read in daylight, warm enough that nothing glares. Body contrast ≈ 9.5:1 — crisp, never the eye-straining 21:1 of black-on-white.

●  Dark — warm low-glare night

Warm charcoal, never blue-black; warm off-white ink, never #fff. Built for night reading — low overall luminance, accents lifted just enough to stay AA, glow pulled almost to nothing.

02 · Color

Quiet color, disciplined chroma

Every value is OKLCH. Neutrals are warm-shifted so the surface reads like paper, not a screen. The signature is a calm spruce-teal — green is the most eye-restful hue — kept at low chroma so it signals without glare. A clay warm accent appears only at the trim.

The chroma rule

Distance from the centre is saturation. Notice the discipline: ink sits near the middle (barely-there warmth), the signature spruce is only moderately out, and the one high-chroma value in the whole system is error — so failure never reads as decoration. Hues cluster in two warm bands (28–62°) and one cool rest-point (168°). Nothing is loud.

Semantic tokens — current theme values (flip the toggle to compare)

The Quiet-Accent Rule

The signature is the only color used for meaning — brand label, focus ring, spinner, links. It never fills a surface or tints body text.

The No-Color-Only Rule

English term vs. its translation is told apart by order, label and weight — never color. Primary users read in a second language.

The Comfort-Contrast Rule

Body text aims for ~8–11:1, not the maximal 21:1. Clearly legible, gentle on the eye over a long article. Verify in-browser, per theme.

06 · Brand mark

Retiring the holly — exploring a theme-neutral mark

The holly is intrinsically festive, so it can't anchor a theme-able brand. These four explorations are all built from the system's own vocabulary — the headword rule and a diacritic accent — so the mark means "a defined word" in any climate. The accent dot is a quiet nod to the diacritics of the reader's own language.

All marks are CSP-safe SVG with fills bound to --ad-accent / --ad-warm, drawn at 18px (trigger) and 21px (card). Always aria-hidden — every placement carries its own text label.

08 · Principles

Do & Don't

Do

  • Keep one quiet accent for meaning — focus ring, spinner, brand, links.
  • Warm-shift every neutral; reach for paper, not white, and charcoal, not black.
  • Aim body contrast at ~8–11:1 — clear but gentle. Verify per theme, in-browser.
  • Keep the card fully opaque & self-contained: own shadow root, own tokens.
  • Read only --ad-* semantic tokens in components.
  • Distinguish the two languages by order, label and weight — never color.
  • Tint shadows warm (45–60° hue); degrade motion under reduced-motion.

Don't

  • Don't reintroduce seasonal/festive trim into the default themes.
  • Don't use pure #fff or #000 anywhere — they glare and fatigue.
  • Don't push the accent to high chroma or let it glow aggressively.
  • Don't fill a surface or tint body text with the accent.
  • Don't nest the card inside a second visible card or frame.
  • Don't hard-code a hex value in a component — add a token instead.
  • Don't sprawl Settings into a dashboard — keep it a calm, restful form even when themed.
