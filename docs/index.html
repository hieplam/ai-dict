The one thing word-only dictionaries can’t do

Ordinary dictionaries look up the word.
This one reads the sentence.

Classic dictionary extensions, including Google’s, throw away everything around your selection and hand back every meaning the word has ever had. You scroll and guess. AI Dictionary sends the word with its sentence, so you get the one sense that’s actually in play, already translated.

"I sit on the grassy bank of the river."

"The next day the bank approved my loan."

Ngân hàng a business that holds money

The full tour

Everything it does, and how to drive it

Define, without leaving

Select a word or phrase. A small Define button appears beside it. Click it and the card opens right there: pronunciation, part of speech, a plain English explanation, your translation, and an example.

  • Press Esc or click away to dismiss and keep reading.
  • Repeated lookups of the same word come from the local cache, instantly.
  • Works on any normal website; Chrome’s own pages are off-limits to all extensions.

The side panel: your reading desk

Click the side-panel button in the card’s header (or the toolbar icon) and the lookup moves into Chrome’s side panel, beside the page instead of on it.

  • The current definition sits on top, your Recent lookups below.
  • Click any Recent entry to bring that definition back into focus.
  • Each row has a delete button that removes the entry and its cached answer, in one tap.

Settings, section by section

Four quiet sections, nothing buried:

  • Connection. Pick your AI provider, Gemini (default, model gemini-2.5-flash) or ChatGPT (gpt-4o-mini). Paste that provider’s key and hit Test connection to confirm it works. Each provider remembers its own key.
  • Translation. Choose your target language and rewrite the Card format: the section list every answer follows. Restore default any time.
  • Appearance. Sepia, Dark, High Contrast, or Match system.
  • Privacy & data. Toggle caching and history, opt in or out of anonymous crash reports (off by default), clear the cache, clear history, or export your history.

Make the card answer your way

The card’s layout is a short text recipe you can edit. The default keeps it to meaning and translation; the demos on this page use a richer five-section recipe with IPA, part of speech, and an example. Placeholders like {target_lang} are filled in per lookup, and your selected word plus its sentence are always sent automatically.

Ask for etymology. Ask for three examples. Ask for gentler English. It’s your dictionary.

Where your data lives: on your device

There is no backend. Everything the extension knows sits in your browser’s local extension storage (chrome.storage.local), and Settings gives you a button for every part of it.

Stored Where Your controls
API key This device only Replace or clear it in Settings
Settings This device only Edit any time; changes apply after saving
Answer cache This device only Toggle off, or Clear cache
Lookup history This device only Toggle off, delete rows, Clear history, or Export

The only thing that ever leaves the browser is the lookup itself, your word, its sentence, and the page title, sent straight to the provider you chose with your own key. Nothing else, to no one else.

Three reading lights

Sepia is warm paper for daytime reads. Dark is a low-glare night surface, never blue-black. High Contrast clears AAA for maximum legibility. Every surface re-themes together: card, panel, settings.

Two minutes, once

Get started

  1. Add it to Chrome

    Install from the Chrome Web Store (works in Edge and Brave too). Prefer to sideload? Every GitHub release ships the same build as dist-chrome.zip.

  2. Get your free Gemini key

    Create one in Google AI Studio: sign in, click Create API key, copy. About a minute, and the free tier covers everyday reading. Prefer ChatGPT? An OpenAI key works too.

  3. Paste, save, read

    The welcome screen opens by itself: pick your reading language, paste the key, Save & activate. The key is stored only in your browser and used only to call your chosen provider.

Straight answers

Questions people actually ask

Is it really free?

The extension is free. Lookups run on your own API key, so you pay your provider only for what you use; Gemini’s free tier is more than enough for everyday reading.

Is my reading private?

There’s no account and no server. Each lookup goes directly from your browser to Google or OpenAI using your key, and the extension’s network access is locked by its Content Security Policy to exactly those two endpoints. History, cache, and key live in local browser storage. The only optional signal is anonymous crash reporting, off by default, and it never includes page content, selected text, full URLs, or your key.

Can my API key leak?

The key is saved in chrome.storage.local on your device and attached only to requests sent to the provider you picked. It never touches any other server, because there isn’t one. Remove it any time by clearing the field in Settings.

Which languages does it translate into?

Vietnamese ships as the default target, with English also built in, and the Card format’s {target_lang} placeholder follows whatever you choose. The source language is English for now.

Does it work in Safari or on my phone?

Not yet. Today it’s Chrome on a computer, plus Chrome-family browsers like Edge and Brave. A Safari and iOS version is planned; watch the GitHub repository for news.

Why did the Define button not appear?

Make sure you selected text on a normal web page. Browser-internal pages like chrome:// and the Web Store are off-limits to all extensions, and freshly installed extensions need a tab reload before they can run there.
